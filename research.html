Hi, I'm Lei Zhong

I am a dedicated researcher passionate about exploring the intersections of technology, society, and human experience. My work seeks to understand how systems evolve, how people interact with their environments, and how we can create more equitable and sustainable futures.

With a background spanning multiple disciplines, I approach research with curiosity and rigor, always asking deeper questions and seeking connections between seemingly disparate fields. My methodology combines quantitative analysis with qualitative insight, creating a holistic understanding of complex phenomena.

Through collaboration, experimentation, and careful observation, I strive to contribute meaningful knowledge that can inform both academic discourse and practical applications in the real world.

Related Works

  • Zhong, L., Yu, D., Research on Vertical Greening Construction Models in High-Density Urban Areas, Harbin Institute of Technology, 2016.
    Undergraduate Research | 2016
    Explored different types of vertical greening and their potential to improve environmental quality in high-density areas, such as in Singapore and Guangzhou.
  • Zhong, L., Tibor, S., Modelling the Response of an Alluvial Multi-Layer Aquifer to Variations in Pumping Rates and Locations Within a Conjunctive Water Supply System – Case Study of Cauca Valley, Colombia, Erasmus Mundus Joint Master Programme, 2018.
    Master's Thesis | 2018
    Applied iMod and Python for groundwater flow modelling and evaluated the impact of the pumping strategies in Cauca Valley, Colombia.
  • Zhong, L., Zoran, V., Francac, M., Combining water engineering and landscape architecture practices for multifunctional and robust design of Nature-Based Solutions, EGU General Assembly 2020
    Conference Paper | 2020
    Presented an interdisciplinary approach to integrating water engineering and landscape design for urban resilience through Nature-Based Solutions
  • Distribution characteristics and protection recommendations of Halophila beccarii seagrass beds in Tangjiawan, Zhuhai
    Paper under Review | 2026
    Conducted field surveys of seagrass beds in densely populated urban coastal areas to assess distribution changes associated with the post-COVID surge in tourism and beach harvesting, and developed evidence-based conservation recommendations
  • Blue Cleaner Program Report on Seabed Debris (2019–2025)
    Report in preparation | 2026
    Analysed seven years of seabed debris removal data to assess waste composition, sources, and trends, and developed environmental management and protection recommendations
